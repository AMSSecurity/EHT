Step 2 — Get the F.A.C.T.S.
Get the F.A.C.T.S. — 3 Steps to Assess Found Property
1st
Find the Owner
  • Interview people in the vicinity
  • Review CCTV
  • Every object has an owner — find the owner and avoid disruption
2nd
Assess the Property
  • Follow the HOTALERTS guidelines above
  • Gather all evidence of the placement of the item, including CCTV showing activities of any person associated with the item
3rd
Consider the Situation
  • Conduct of the owner
  • Environmental factors
  • Other suspicious factors
  • Information from authorities
Step 3 — Act on Assessment Results
✔ If the Item Is NOT Suspicious
  • Review CCTV to satisfy yourself this was not a reconnaissance exercise
  • If suspicious activity identified, report it to the local police, Crime Stoppers or the National Security Hotline
  • If an owner cannot be identified, remove the article and follow lost property procedures
  • Consider submitting an intelligence report to police
⚠ If You Are Undecided
  • Do not TOUCH, TILT or TAMPER
  • Do not use mobile phones or radios within 25 metres (electronic frequencies may cause a device to detonate)
  • Inform the supervisor
  • Cordon off the area and move people away
  • If chemical, biological, radioactive or nuclear (CBRN) suspected — evacuate, isolate exposed persons, turn off heating, ventilation and cooling (HVAC)
  • Advise security who will assess the need to alert emergency services
  • Call Triple Zero (000) and follow their instruction
🚨 If Assessed as Suspicious
  • Do not TOUCH, TILT or TAMPER
  • Do not use mobile phones or radios within 25 metres radius (electronic frequencies may cause a device to detonate)
  • If the suspicious item is a vehicle — evacuate at least 500 metres
  • If CBRN suspected — evacuate, isolate exposed persons, turn off HVAC
  • Cordon off immediate area and move people away
  • Call Triple Zero (000)
  • Advise security
  • Respond to the directions of emergency services
  • Gather all evidence of placement of item including CCTV
  • Evacuate area at least 100 metres from the item
