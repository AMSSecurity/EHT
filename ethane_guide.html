Emergency Reporting

ETHANE — Emergency Information Guide

Relay the important information required by emergency services
⚠ Life-threatening situations — call 000 immediately

The ETHANE mnemonic assists staff to relay the important information required by emergency services when reporting a critical incident. Work through each element in order to ensure nothing is missed.

E
Element 1Exact Location
  • Confirm nearest junction or exact address
  • Geographical size of the incident
T
Element 2Type of Incident
  • Explosion, building collapse, firearms incident etc.
H
Element 3Hazards
  • Identify the hazards present or suspected (such as number of hostiles, types of weapons etc.)
  • Consider potential or secondary devices
  • Is evacuation or in-vacuation necessary and safe?
A
Element 4Access Routes
  • Nominate and search the rendezvous point
  • Clarify routes which are blocked
  • Update with routes that are safe to use
N
Element 5Number of Casualties
  • List type and severity
  • Approximate number of dead, injured, survivors and witnesses
E
Element 6Emergency Services
  • List those services present and those required
  • Conduct a joint dynamic hazard assessment with the emergency services
