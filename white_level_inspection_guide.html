White Level Inspection Guide

Staff Reference — Your response will depend on the circumstances
🔍

How Are White Level Inspections Conducted?

A white level inspection is an inspection by all staff members of their respective workplace for any articles that are unusual, suspicious, or unable to be accounted for.

Important: It is not just a search for bombs.
❓

Why Are They Necessary?

White level inspections are conducted so that all staff and management can be confident their workplace is secure. A secure workplace will:

  • Minimise potential disruptions to work activities and productivity
  • Assist police and emergency services to determine the most appropriate response to an unattended item
👥

Who Conducts a White Level Inspection?

The people in the best position to conduct white level inspections are the people who know and work within the area.

Staff are assigned to inspect their own workplace area, as well as common areas such as foyers, bathrooms, and parking areas.

📋

Where Are White Level Inspections Conducted?

A white level inspection plan devised by management should cover all areas of the workplace and take into account variables such as:

  • Shift considerations, emergency maintenance and cleaning schedules
  • Staff will inspect their own workplace area and nominated staff should be assigned to inspect common areas, such as foyers, bathrooms and parking areas to ensure they are not overlooked
🕐

When Are White Level Inspections Conducted?

White level inspections should be undertaken:

📅
Routinely
Each day upon arrival at work
🎲
Randomly
As determined by the manager
📣
On Request
At the request of management
✅

How Are White Level Inspections Conducted?

When conducting a white level inspection the following steps should be undertaken:

  1. 1
    Conduct a visual check of the route to your work area and work station. Remember to check all drawers and cupboards that you are responsible for.
  2. 2
    In addition to your own work area, inspect any other areas that may have been assigned to you — for example, a bathroom or kitchen.
  3. 3
    Once your inspection has been completed, report to your supervisor or manager. You may then resume normal duties.
  4. 4
    Supervisors will then report the results of the inspection in accordance with established procedures. A record should be kept of the inspection including the area where an inspection was conducted, the date and the outcome of the inspection.
🚨

What Do I Do If I Find an Unattended Article?

If the article does not belong where you found it — stop your inspection. Take actions to identify whether the article is suspicious using the F.A.C.T.S. method:

F
Find the Owner
Who put it there? Check CCTV, interview people in the vicinity. Every item has an owner.
A
Assess the Property
HOTALERTS — evaluate the physical characteristics of the item.
C
Consider the Situation
Relevance is the conduct of a suspect, suspicious circumstances, information from authorities.
T
Think
Environmental factors: current events, time of day, day of week, holiday, anniversary of other terrorist attacks.
S
Suspicious?
Get the F.A.C.T.S. — determine whether the article is suspicious and act accordingly.
Reminder: Life-threatening situations must be reported to the police immediately by calling emergency services.
